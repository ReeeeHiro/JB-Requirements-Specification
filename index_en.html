Overview

This document defines the requirements for the PCB and peripheral circuits of the junction block (JB) for the next prototype vehicle of hop on. Please use this as a design request document for PICLITE ENTERPRISE CORP., a PCB manufacturer and JB supplier.

03

System Preconditions

Power supply configuration
  • Main power source: 72V LiFePO4 battery (operating voltage range: 60–85V).
  • A DCDC converter steps down 72V to 13–14V. Output: 50–100W, with possible future expansion up to 200W.
  • The DCDC converter is a simple 4-terminal type: 72V_IN / GND / 12V_OUT / GND. No ENABLE or PGOOD terminal.
Power scale of this JB
  • This JB does not include high-power circuits related to motor control or the main battery power line.
  • Current margin shall be considered in the design because the current consumption may increase if optional electrical accessories such as a USB charger or cigarette lighter socket are added.
Lighting load specification
  • Design shall assume LED loads. Changing to filament bulbs is not recommended and, if performed, shall be at the user's own responsibility.
  • Inrush current tolerance when filament bulbs are used is outside the warranty scope of the JB.

06

Selection Requirements for Each Component

IG relay
  • Select a general 12V automotive-grade relay (μISO or equivalent).
  • The coil drive power shall be the 12V system. The contact capacity shall include sufficient margin against the total IG-linked load current.
Headlight relay (full specification only)
  • Select the same or equivalent part as the IG relay.
  • The handlebar switch (LightSW) shall control only the relay coil, preventing direct current flow through the switch contacts.
  • It shall be linked with the IG-linked system (LightSW ON position).
Flasher relay
  • Use a universal fixed-frequency type that does not depend on connected load.
  • Must satisfy JASO D619 (60–120 flashes/min).
  • Current-sensing type (bulb-out detection type) is not allowed because it is not compatible with diode parallel-branch circuits.
  • If another method can reduce cost, please propose it separately.
FET for IG-linked turn signal control
  • Use an N-channel MOSFET.
  • Drain: flasher relay output (+B main line) side; Source: turn switch / LED side; Gate: downstream of IG relay contact.
  • Provide a gate resistor (Rg) and gate-source resistor (Rgs). Constants will be provided separately.
  • Place a Schottky diode (D_iso) downstream of the source for reverse-current prevention and Vf potential-difference adjustment. Use the same type as the hazard-system diodes.
  • Please select the specific device based on the assumed specification below. If selection or implementation is difficult, please contact us separately.

PCB Design Requirements

  • Provide voltage-measurement test pads before and after each device and power path.
  • Provide solderable through-hole pads at both ends of spare fuse and spare diode circuits.
  • DNP components shall have patterns only and be compatible with post-process mounting.
  • If changes to our requirements provide clear cost or manufacturability advantages due to PCB pattern constraints, such as reducing spare fuse count to reduce PCB area, please actively propose them.
  • Design temperature range: -30 to +50°C, same as operating environment.
10

Vehicle-side Connection Specifications and Service Terminal

Vehicle-side connection specification
  • Connector symbols in the circuit diagram represent the connection method; adoption of connectors is not mandatory.
  • Please propose connection solutions that your company can support, such as terminal blocks, faston terminals, or direct wire connection. Final specification will be determined through separate discussion.
Service terminal (External Battery Terminal)
  • Optional terminal for connecting a small lead-acid battery, such as a motorcycle battery, as a countermeasure against voltage fluctuation when filament bulbs are used.
  • In the standard LED specification, this terminal remains unused. If through-hole, it may remain as is; if terminal-block type, pads suitable for post-processing are preferred.
  • The connected battery is naturally charged by the DCDC converter output voltage (13–14V). Active charge-control circuitry is not required.
